1. Introduction to Areas

This chapter focuses on calculating areas of parallelograms and triangles, particularly when they share the same base and lie between the same parallel lines. These concepts are fundamental for understanding geometric relationships.

üìñ Basic Concepts

Area: The measure of the surface enclosed by a closed figure.

Congruent Figures: Have equal areas (and same shape/size).

Equal Figures: Have equal areas (but may have different shapes).

Note: All congruent figures are equal, but not all equal figures are congruent.

2. Figures on the Same Base and Between Same Parallels

üìñ Definition

Two figures are on the same base and between the same parallels if:

‚Ä¢ They have a common base (side)

‚Ä¢ The vertices opposite to the base lie on a line parallel to the base

2.1 Key Understanding

  • The base is the common side shared by both figures.
  • The opposite vertices must lie on a line parallel to this base.
  • The perpendicular distance between the two parallel lines is the height.
  • Figures with the same base and between same parallels have equal areas.

3. Area of Parallelograms

‚ö†Ô∏è Area Formula for Parallelogram

Area = Base √ó Height

Where height is the perpendicular distance between the base and its opposite side.

3.1 Important Theorems on Parallelograms

‚ö†Ô∏è Theorem 1

Parallelograms on the same base and between the same parallels are equal in area.

Area of parallelogram = Base √ó Height (perpendicular distance between parallels)

‚ö†Ô∏è Theorem 2

If a parallelogram and a rectangle are on the same base and between the same parallels, they have equal areas.

üìù Example: Parallelogram Area

Q: A parallelogram has a base of 12 cm and height of 8 cm. Find its area.

Solution:

Area = Base √ó Height

= 12 √ó 8

= 96 cm¬≤


Q: Two parallelograms ABCD and ABEF are on the same base AB and between same parallels AB and CF. If area of ABCD = 60 cm¬≤, what is area of ABEF?

Solution:

By Theorem 1, parallelograms on same base and between same parallels have equal areas.

Area of ABEF = 60 cm¬≤

4. Area of Triangles

‚ö†Ô∏è Area Formula for Triangle

Area = ¬Ω √ó Base √ó Height

Where height is the perpendicular distance from the base to the opposite vertex.

4.1 Important Theorems on Triangles

‚ö†Ô∏è Theorem 3

Triangles on the same base and between the same parallels are equal in area.

‚ö†Ô∏è Theorem 4

The area of a triangle is half the area of a parallelogram on the same base and between the same parallels.

Area of ‚ñ≥ = ¬Ω √ó Area of parallelogram (when on same base and between same parallels)

üìù Example: Triangle Area

Q: A triangle has base 10 cm and height 6 cm. Find its area.

Solution:

Area = ¬Ω √ó Base √ó Height

= ¬Ω √ó 10 √ó 6

= ¬Ω √ó 60

= 30 cm¬≤


Q: A parallelogram ABCD and triangle ABE have the same base AB and are between same parallels. If area of parallelogram is 80 cm¬≤, find area of triangle.

Solution:

By Theorem 4:

Area of triangle = ¬Ω √ó Area of parallelogram

= ¬Ω √ó 80

= 40 cm¬≤

4.2 Relationship Between Triangle and Parallelogram

  • A diagonal divides a parallelogram into two triangles of equal area.
  • Each triangle has area = ¬Ω √ó area of the parallelogram.
  • Triangles on same base and between same parallels are equal in area.
  • Two triangles with equal bases and equal heights have equal areas.

5. Median and Area of Triangle

‚ö†Ô∏è Theorem 5: Median Divides Triangle into Equal Areas

A median of a triangle divides it into two triangles of equal area.

Each smaller triangle has area = ¬Ω √ó area of original triangle.

5.1 Properties of Median

  • A median connects a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.
  • A triangle has three medians.
  • Each median divides the triangle into two triangles of equal area.
  • The three medians divide a triangle into six smaller triangles of equal area.

üìù Example: Median and Area

Solution:

AD is a median (connects vertex A to midpoint D of BC)

By median theorem, AD divides triangle into two equal areas

Area of ‚ñ≥ABD = ¬Ω √ó Area of ‚ñ≥ABC

= ¬Ω √ó 48

üîë Key Points to Remember

  • Height must be perpendicular to the base
  • Same base + same parallels ‚Üí equal areas
  • Triangle area = ¬Ω √ó base √ó height
  • Parallelogram area = base √ó height
  • Median creates two equal areas
  • Congruent figures have equal areas, but equal areas doesn't mean congruent

Problem-solving strategy

üí° Study Tips for Areas

‚Ä¢ Always draw clear diagrams showing base and height.

‚Ä¢ Mark parallel lines clearly on your diagrams.

‚Ä¢ Remember: height is ALWAYS perpendicular to the base.

‚Ä¢ "Same base and between same parallels" is a key phrase - memorize what it means!

‚Ä¢ Triangle area is always half of parallelogram area (same base, same parallels).

‚Ä¢ Don't confuse slant height with perpendicular height.

‚Ä¢ Practice identifying which figures share the same base and parallels.

‚Ä¢ Median problems are common - remember it divides area equally!

üîë Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using slant side instead of perpendicular height
  • Forgetting to multiply by ¬Ω for triangle areas
  • Confusing "equal areas" with "congruent figures"
  • Not identifying the correct base and height
  • Assuming different-looking figures can't have equal areas
  • Forgetting that median creates TWO equal areas (not just divides)
