🛡️ Cyber Safety and Ethics

1. Digital Footprint

A digital footprint is the trail of data you create while using the internet. It includes websites you visit, emails you send, and information you submit to online services.

  • Active Digital Footprint: Data you intentionally submit online (e.g., social media posts).
  • Passive Digital Footprint: Data collected without your explicit knowledge (e.g., IP address, browsing history via cookies).

2. Cyber Crimes and Threats

Common Threats

Malware: Malicious software designed to cause harm. Includes Viruses (attach to files), Worms (self-replicating), and Trojans (disguised as useful software).

Phishing: Fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information (passwords, credit cards) by disguising as a trustworthy entity in an electronic communication.

Cyberbullying: The use of electronic communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating or threatening nature.

Hacking: Unauthorized access to computer systems or networks.

3. Safe Online Practices

To protect your digital identity and data, follow these best practices:

  • Use strong, unique passwords (mix of letters, numbers, symbols).
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  • Do not click on suspicious links or download unknown attachments.
  • Keep your operating system and antivirus software updated.
  • Use secure, encrypted connections (HTTPS) when entering personal information.

4. Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

IPR refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions, literary and artistic works, designs, and symbols.

Types of IPR

Copyright: Protects original works of authorship (books, music, software).

Patent: Protects inventions and gives the owner exclusive rights to make, use, or sell it.

Trademark: Protects logos, names, or symbols that distinguish goods/services of one enterprise from another.

Plagiarism: Presenting someone else's work or ideas as your own without proper consent or citation. It is an ethical violation.
